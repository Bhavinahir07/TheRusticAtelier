For the lemon cake:
    2 ½ cups all-purpose flour
    2 tsp baking powder
    ½ tsp baking soda
    ½ tsp salt
    1 cup unsalted butter (softened to room temperature)
    1 ¾ cups granulated sugar
    4 large eggs (room temperature)
    1 cup buttermilk (room temperature)
    ¼ cup vegetable oil
    3 tbsp fresh lemon zest (from about 3 lemons)
    ¼ cup fresh lemon juice
    1 tsp vanilla extract
For the lemon cream cheese frosting:
    8 oz block cream cheese (softened)
    ½ cup unsalted butter (softened)
    3–4 cups powdered sugar (sifted)
    2 tbsp fresh lemon juice
    1 tsp lemon zest
    1 tsp vanilla extract

1. Prepare the cake pans and oven

    Preheat oven to 350°F (175°C).

    Grease and flour two 9-inch round cake pans.

    Tip: For guaranteed easy release, line the bottom of the pans with parchment paper after greasing.

2. Mix the dry ingredients

    In a medium bowl, whisk together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt.

    Set aside.

3. Cream the butter, sugar, and zest

    In a large mixing bowl, beat the softened butter, sugar, and lemon zest together on medium-high speed for 3-4 minutes until very light and fluffy.

    Comment: Beating the zest with the butter and sugar helps to release the oils from the zest, infusing the cake with a stronger lemon flavor.

4. Add eggs and liquid ingredients

    Add the room temperature eggs one at a time, beating well after each addition.

    In a separate bowl or measuring cup, whisk together the buttermilk, vegetable oil, lemon juice, and vanilla extract.

    Tip: Room temperature ingredients emulsify better, creating a more uniform and tender cake crumb.

5. Alternate adding dry and wet mixtures

    With the mixer on low speed, add one-third of the dry mixture, followed by half of the wet mixture. Repeat, ending with the dry mixture.

    Mix only until just combined.

    Comment: Overmixing develops gluten and can result in a dense, heavy cake. A few flour streaks are okay.

6. Bake the cakes

    Divide the batter evenly between the prepared pans and smooth the tops.

    Bake for 30–35 minutes, or until a toothpick inserted into the center comes out clean with a few moist crumbs.

    Cool in the pans for 15 minutes, then invert onto a wire rack to cool completely.

7. Make the lemon cream cheese frosting

    In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter together until smooth and creamy.

    Gradually beat in the powdered sugar on low speed. Once incorporated, add the lemon juice, zest, and vanilla.

    Increase speed to high and beat for 2-3 minutes until the frosting is light and fluffy.

8. Assemble the cake

    Place one cooled cake layer on a serving plate.

    Spread about 1.5 cups of frosting evenly over the top.

    Place the second layer on top and frost the top and sides of the entire cake.

    Tip: For the neatest slices, chill the frosted cake for about 30 minutes before serving to allow the frosting to set.

Recipe Instructions – Confetti Cake
1. Oven and Pan Preparation

Position a rack in the center of your oven and preheat to 350°F (175°C).

Thoroughly butter two 9-inch round cake pans. Dust them with flour, tapping out any excess. For a flawless release, place a parchment paper circle on the bottom of each pan.

2. Sift the Dry Components

In a large bowl, sift together the flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Sifting is not just for mixing; it aerates the flour, which is key to a light, fluffy cake.

Set your sifted mixture aside for now.

3. Create the Base Batter

In the bowl of a stand mixer fitted with the paddle attachment, beat the softened butter, vegetable oil, and granulated sugar on medium-high speed.

Continue for 3-4 minutes until the mixture is significantly lighter in color and has a creamy, whipped texture.

Comment: The combination of butter for rich flavor and oil for lasting moisture is the secret to the perfect confetti cake texture.

4. Incorporate Eggs and Flavoring

Add the room temperature eggs individually, allowing each one to fully blend into the batter before adding the next.

Mix in the vanilla extract.

Tip: Scraping down the bowl with a spatula after adding the eggs ensures that no heavy batter is left at the bottom.

5. Build the Cake Batter

Reduce the mixer speed to low. Add about a third of your dry ingredients, followed by half of the buttermilk.

Repeat this alternating process, finishing with the last of the dry ingredients. Mix only until you no longer see white streaks of flour.

6. Add the Sprinkles

Remove the bowl from the mixer and use a rubber spatula to gently fold in the rainbow jimmies.

Tip: Use long, jimmie-style sprinkles. The small, round nonpareils are more likely to dissolve and bleed their color throughout the batter during baking. Fold them in with just a few turns of the spatula to minimize color streaking.

7. Bake to Perfection

Carefully divide the finished batter between your two prepared pans, smoothing the tops with your spatula.

Bake for 30–35 minutes. The cakes are done when they are golden, and a wooden skewer inserted into the center comes out clean.

Let the cakes rest in their pans on a wire rack for about 15 minutes. This allows them to set before you invert them out of the pans to cool completely.

8. Whip up the Buttercream

In a large, clean bowl, beat the softened butter with a mixer for about 2 minutes until it's smooth and pale.

With the mixer on low, gradually add the sifted powdered sugar. Once it's mostly incorporated, add the vanilla and salt.

Increase the speed to medium-high and whip for another 3-4 minutes. Drizzle in the heavy cream as it whips, until the frosting is light, airy, and easily spreadable.

9. Assemble Your Masterpiece

Place one fully cooled cake layer onto your cake stand or serving plate.

Apply a generous layer of buttercream on top, spreading it evenly.

Carefully position the second cake layer on top, then apply a thin "crumb coat" of frosting over the entire cake. Chill for 15 minutes before applying the final, thicker layer of frosting.

Comment: The crumb coat is a thin layer of frosting that traps any loose crumbs, ensuring your final coat of frosting is clean and beautiful.

Recipe Instructions – Lemon Cake
1. Pre-Bake Setup

Adjust an oven rack to the middle position and preheat to 350°F (175°C).

Prepare two 9-inch round cake pans by greasing them with butter or baking spray and lightly flouring them.

2. Combine Dry Ingredients

In a medium bowl, use a whisk to combine the all-purpose flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt. Whisking serves to both mix and aerate the ingredients.

3. Infuse the Sugar with Lemon

In a large mixing bowl, combine the granulated sugar and fresh lemon zest. Use your fingertips to rub the zest into the sugar for about a minute.

Add the softened butter and beat on medium-high speed for 3-4 minutes until the mixture is pale, fluffy, and highly fragrant.

Comment: Rubbing the zest into the sugar releases the lemon's essential oils, resulting in a much more aromatic and flavorful cake.

4. Integrate the Wet Ingredients

Add the room temperature eggs one by one, ensuring each is fully incorporated before adding the next.

In a separate liquid measuring cup, stir together the buttermilk, vegetable oil, fresh lemon juice, and vanilla.

Tip: For maximum juice, firmly roll your lemons on the countertop before cutting and squeezing them.

5. Form the Final Batter

Turn your mixer to its lowest speed. Add the flour mixture in three parts, alternating with the buttermilk mixture in two parts (begin and end with the flour mixture).

Mix until the last streaks of flour have just disappeared. Do not overmix.

6. Bake the Cakes

Pour the batter evenly into your prepared pans and use a spatula to level the surface.

Bake for 30–35 minutes. You'll know the cakes are ready when a toothpick inserted into their centers emerges with a few moist crumbs attached.

Allow the cakes to cool in their pans for 15 minutes before turning them out onto a cooling rack to cool the rest of the way.

7. Prepare the Lemon Frosting

In a large bowl, beat the softened cream cheese and butter with an electric mixer until there are absolutely no lumps.

Slowly beat in the sifted powdered sugar. Once combined, add the fresh lemon juice, lemon zest, and vanilla.

Increase the mixer speed to high and whip for 2-3 minutes until the frosting is exceptionally smooth and airy.

8. Construct the Cake

Once the cake layers are completely cool to the touch, place the first layer on a serving platter.

Spread a thick, even layer of the lemon cream cheese frosting over it.

Gently place the second layer on top. Frost the top and sides of the cake smoothly with the remaining frosting.

Tip: A chilled cake is easier to frost. Consider refrigerating the layers for 15-20 minutes before you begin assembling.
